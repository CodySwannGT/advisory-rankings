$6B Morgan Stanley Team Jumps to Wells Fargo Advisors in NYC

by Karmen Alexander
May 1, 2026
The Taylor Group, which joined Wells Fargo from Morgan Stanley on May 1.

A fast-growing Morgan Stanley team that managed $5.94 billion in assets jumped to Wells Fargo Advisors in New York City, according to a spokesperson for the hiring firm.

The Taylor Group at Morgan Stanley, which is led by 16-year broker C. James Taylor, produced $18.6 million in annual revenue, the Wells spokesperson said. The team reports to Michael Freiheit, branch manager at Wells’ Midtown Manhattan office in the GM building, and Patrick Baumann, New York City market leader. 

“This is a highly driven team that operates with precision and purpose,” Baumann said in a statement. “They’re keen to take advantage of Wells Fargo’s full balance sheet, which gives them a clear runway to serve their clients holistically.”

The 19-person team includes advisors Shane Drumm, Michaella Irvine, Cameron Irvine, Marcus Briscoe, Jamison Embury, Roger McGlynn, Hunter Embury and Kyle Drumm as well as 10 support staff. 

Their practice had ballooned in recent years as the group managed $1.2 billion in 2023, according to a Barron’s profile that year. 

A source at a competing firm called the team “high quality” and said that their growth in assets was driven in part by their concentration of clients who are employees and executives at Nvidia. The chipmaker’s stock has surged more than 1,200% over the past five years. 

Taylor first registered with Hennion & Walsh in 2009 and worked at Merrill Lynch for nine years before moving to Morgan Stanley in 2020, according to BrokerCheck. He did not respond to a request for comment. 

A Wells spokesperson also did not return a request for comment regarding the team’s client base. 

A Morgan Stanley spokesperson did not immediately provide a comment. The firm has been leaning into its workplace business, including servicing the corporate stock plans for Fortune 500 companies, as a way to funnel clients to its advisors. 

Meanwhile, Wells has been aggressively pricing deals for large, high-growth teams, recruiters have said. Its offers for a group like Taylor’s could include 275% of trailing-12 revenue in upfront cash, which would put it at or near the top of the market not including back-end bonuses, the source said. 

Wells Fargo executives earlier this month attributed the bank’s recruiting success to its multi-channel approach, including its FiNet independent brokerage and RIA custody unit launching later this year. Roger Gershman, an industry recruiter who was not involved in the move but was familiar with the team, claimed they narrowed their search to three firms but wanted a place that would provide a long-term path to independence.

“We’ve talked a lot about the momentum that we’re building there,” Chief Financial Officer Mike Santomassimo said earlier in April about the wealth division, which is led by Barry Sommers. “That business just grows through improving just naturally the net flows we’re getting [and] the recruiting we’re seeing.”

The wirehouse earlier this week reeled in a UBS Wealth Management USA team managing $466 million in Florida. In April, a UBS team with $2.1 billion joined Wells’ independent Financial Network. 

–Mason Braswell contributed to this story.
